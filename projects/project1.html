ABOUT THE PROJECT

I designed a desktop-mounted machine for ATU Sligo to solve the high cost of manufacturer-specific filament spools. The system allows the transfer of affordable 3rd-party filament onto proprietary spools. To ensure the reliability of the drive system, I integrated a custom MATLAB analysis module to determine the optimal gear material between PMMA and PLA.

Key Technical Features

  • Power Transmission: Integrated an Oriental Motor 25W induction motor (4IK25GN-CW2TE) with a 4GN120RA gearhead providing a 120:1 reduction ratio.
  • Mechanism Design: Developed a reciprocating filament guide to ensure even distribution across the spool width.
  • Computational Analysis: Built a MATLAB script to calculate gear bending failure thresholds using the Lewis Bending Equation.

Technical Deep-Dive: The MATLAB Analysis

The Challenge: The drive gears need to withstand the high torque generated by the 120:1 gearhead. I used MATLAB to simulate the stress on gear teeth to choose between PMMA and PLA.

MATLAB Logic: The script iterates through various torque loads and compares the calculated Sigma against the Tensile Yield Strength of PMMA (approx. 70 MPa) and PLA (approx. 50 MPa). My analysis determined if the factor of safety was sufficient for the high-torque start-up phase of the induction motor.
