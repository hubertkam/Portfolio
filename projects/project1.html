Filament Spool Re-Wind Machine

Mechanical Design & MATLAB Gear Analysis

1 / 5

PROJECT INFO

Year 2025
Role Mechanical Systems Designer
Tools SolidWorks, MATLAB

PROJECT SUMMARY

Designed a compact, desktop-mounted filament re-wind machine for ATU Sligo to eliminate dependence on high-cost manufacturer-specific 3D-printer spools. The system enables low-cost third-party filament to be accurately transferred onto proprietary spools while maintaining controlled tension and uniform winding.

Mechanical Design

  • Integrated a 25 W Oriental Motor induction motor (4IK25GN-CW2TE) with a 120:1 gearhead (4GN120RA) to deliver high torque at low speed.
  • Designed the gear-driven power transmission and structural layout for a minimal footprint and desktop mounting.
  • Developed a mechanically linked reciprocating filament guide to ensure even filament distribution across the spool width.
  • Designed all components for manufacturability and compatibility with standard off-the-shelf bearings and gears.

Engineering Analysis (MATLAB)

Built a custom MATLAB tool to evaluate gear tooth bending failure using the Lewis bending equation with realistic load factors. Compared PMMA vs PLA gears under identical geometry and loading conditions.

Analysis Results:

  • PMMA max torque before bending failure: ~63.4 N·m
  • PLA max torque before bending failure: ~52.9 N·m
  • ~20% higher torque capacity for PMMA

PMMA was selected to provide sufficient margin during high-torque motor start-up.

Outcome

  • Delivered a mechanically robust, low-cost solution aligned with university maintenance constraints.
  • Replaced subjective material choice with quantitative, simulation-backed engineering justification.
  • Demonstrated end-to-end ownership: concept → CAD → mechanical analysis → design decision.
